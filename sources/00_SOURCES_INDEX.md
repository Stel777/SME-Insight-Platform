## Key Numbers by Topic (dashboard-ready)

### Structure
- Greek SMEs: 99.8% of all enterprises
- Micro-firm share: **96.4%** (highest in EU)
- SME private-sector employment: **86.4%**
- Total SME employment: **~2.21 million**
- Total SME value added: **~€42.8 billion**

### Performance (2024)
- SME real value added growth: **+0.6%** overall; **+6.8%** micro only
- SME employment growth: **+2.9%** overall; **+6.5%** micro only
- Small firm value added: **-4.1%** (contracting)
- Productivity gap vs OECD: **40% below** OECD average (2023)

### Digital
- Greek SMEs at basic digital intensity: **53%** (EU avg: 73%) — 2024 data
- Greek enterprises with very low DII: **56.2%** — 2023 data
- EU 2030 target for SMEs: **90%** at basic level
- Greece rank: **2nd from bottom** in EU

### Finance
- EIF guarantees to Greek SMEs (2024): **€152 million**
- EIB guarantees to Greek SMEs (2024): **€550 million**
- New SME lending 2024 vs 2019: **Higher** (positive outlier in EU)
- SME lending flow change: **+1.8 pp** over period

### Sentiment (NBG H2 2025)
- SME Confidence Index: **17 points** (above 10-yr average)
- Growth-strategy businesses: **58%** (up from 54%)
- Liquidity constraints: **~10%** (near historic low)
- Significant tariff impact reported: **~33%** (vs 82% in 2021 crisis)

### ESG (NBG May 2025 — 600 SMEs)
- Active ESG interest: **>66%**
- Received substantial ESG info: **only 22%**
- Already implementing ESG: **12%**
- Holistic ESG strategy (incl. governance): **only 3%**
- No mandatory regulation needed to act: **93%**

## Data Reliability Notes

- All 5 sources are institutional/governmental — no consulting blogs or LinkedIn content
- NBG data is the most Greece-specific and most recent (H2 2025)
- EC Fact Sheet is the most authoritative on SME structure
- Eurostat DII is the definitive source on digital intensity — note the 2023 vs 2024 data year difference (see Source 04 notes)
- OECD Financing 2026 is the most recent international scoreboard — March 2026 publication
