## 1. The Core Structural Problem: Firm Size

> Greece's business sector is dominated by micro and small firms in a way that is **unusual even by EU standards**.

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| Micro firm share of business employment | **~47%** |
| Business sector characterisation | Dominated by micro and small firms |
| Productivity gap vs OECD average (2023) | **40% below** OECD average |

- Micro and small firms "often struggle to seize the opportunities of new technologies, innovate and grow"
- The productivity gap is "visible in all sectors of the economy"
- Boosting firm growth and innovation is identified as a **key challenge** for long-term living standards

---

## 2. Access to Finance

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| Primary mechanism | Loan-guarantee and subsidised lending programmes |
| EU fund deployment | NextGenerationEU loans on-lent for private investment |
| Key gap | No robust evaluation mechanism to prevent misallocation to low-productivity enterprises |
| Non-performing loans | Sizeable exposures remain in banking and loan servicing sector |

- Access to finance remains a **key stated barrier** for Greek SMEs alongside skill shortages
- High reliance on bank financing (equity markets underdeveloped for SMEs)

---

## 3. Digital Adoption

- "Significant skill shortages have emerged" preventing firms from "implementing new technologies"
- Low investment in digital tools historically linked to micro-firm dominance and bandwidth constraints
- Investment "has just started to rebound" following the post-2008 decline

---

## 4. Regulatory Environment

| Indicator | Finding |
|---|---|
| PMR improvement 2018–23 | **Largest improvement** in the OECD Product Market Regulation indicator |
| Remaining bottleneck | Restrictive regulations persist in **professional services** |
| Regulatory burden impact | Disproportionately affects micro-firms with no dedicated compliance resource |

---

## 5. Labour Market & Skills

| Indicator | Value / Finding |
|---|---|
| Unemployment rate (Oct 2024) | **9.8%** |
| Skills paradox | High unemployment coexists with significant skill shortages |
| Root cause | Workers lack the specific competencies firms require |
| Impact on SMEs | Skill shortages prevent growth and technology adoption |

---

## 6. Innovation & R&D

- R&D expenditure remains low despite recent progress
- Main innovation barriers:
  1. Weak competition in key sectors
  2. High regulatory burdens
  3. Difficulties obtaining external financing
  4. Labour and skill shortages

---

## 7. Key OECD Recommendations (SME-Relevant)

1. Improve access to finance for SMEs, particularly equity and growth capital
2. Strengthen evaluation of guarantee schemes to direct capital to productive firms
3. Reduce remaining regulatory bottlenecks in professional services
4. Address skills gaps through targeted training and education reform
5. Support technology adoption in micro-firms through scoped, practical programmes
