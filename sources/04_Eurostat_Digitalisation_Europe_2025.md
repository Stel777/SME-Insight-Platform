## 1. The Digital Intensity Index (DII) — Methodology

The DII measures enterprise use of **12 digital technologies**, including:
- AI adoption
- E-sales and e-commerce
- Cloud computing
- ERP/CRM systems
- Advanced analytics
- Cybersecurity tools
- Digital communication platforms

**Scoring:**
- **Very low:** 0–3 technologies used
- **Low:** 4–5 technologies used (= "basic level" minimum)
- **High:** 6–8 technologies used
- **Very high:** 9–12 technologies used

A minimum score of **4 (low or above)** = basic digital intensity (EU 2030 target threshold)

## 5. Root Causes of Low Digital Intensity in Greek SMEs (Eurostat/EC context)

1. **Micro-firm dominance** — 96.4% of firms are micro; digital tools require minimum operational scale to yield ROI
2. **Cost and complexity** — Perceived implementation cost and operational complexity disproportionately affect micro-firms
3. **Bandwidth constraint** — No dedicated IT resource in most Greek SMEs
4. **Skills gap** — Lack of digital skills, confirmed by OECD skill shortage data
5. **Low competition pressure** — In protected sectors, digital adoption is not survival-critical

## 8. Data Notes & Caveats

- The **56.2% very-low** figure relates to 2023 enterprise data (all firms, published in 2024 Eurostat report)
- The **53% basic level** (SMEs only) is from the 2025 Eurostat Digitalisation publication (2024 survey data)
- Both figures are from official Eurostat surveys — they measure slightly different populations (all enterprises vs SMEs only) and different years — hence the apparent difference
- Use 53% (basic level, SMEs, 2024) for dashboard headline as it is the most current and SME-specific
