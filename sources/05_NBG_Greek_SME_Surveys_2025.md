## Part A — SME Confidence Index (H2 2025)

*Published February–March 2026. Covers July–December 2025.*

### 1. Headline Confidence Metric

| Metric | H1 2025 | H2 2025 | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| SME Confidence Index | 16 points | **17 points** | ↑ Improving |
| Businesses pursuing growth strategies | 54% | **58%** | ↑ Improving |
| Liquidity constraints (severe) | ~10% | **~10% (near historic low)** | = Stable |

> The confidence index at 17 points is described as "well above the historical average of the past decade" — a significant signal of cautious structural optimism despite global uncertainty.

---

### 2. Macroeconomic Context (H2 2025)

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| Global Uncertainty Index (Sept 2025) | **600% above long-term average** |
| Global GDP forecast revised to | **3.3%** (up from 2.8% in April 2025) |
| SMEs facing significant tariff pressure | **~33%** |
| SMEs significantly impacted in 2021 energy crisis (comparison) | **82%** |
| SMEs significantly impacted during 2020–2024 supply chain disruptions | **50–60%** |

**Interpretation:** Greek SMEs showed **notable resilience** in H2 2025 — impact from global tariff turbulence was significantly lower than during prior crises.

---

### 3. Response to Tariff / Cost Pressures

Among the ~33% of SMEs reporting significant tariff impact:

| Response | Share of affected firms |
|---|---|
| Experienced cost increases via supply chain | **83%** |
| Identified direct/indirect demand impacts | **17%** |
| Reacted strategically (total) | **22%** |
| — of which: strengthened competitiveness | 8% |
| — of which: found new suppliers or customers | 14% |
| Adopted cost pass-through to customers | **15%** (23% among high-pressure firms) |
| Absorbed costs via reduced profit margins | **~66% (two-thirds)** |

**Key insight:** Most SMEs absorb cost shocks rather than pass them on — this compresses margins and reduces investment capacity, creating latent demand for productivity and efficiency advisory.

---

### 4. Financial Health Indicators

| Indicator | Status |
|---|---|
| Severe liquidity constraints | ~10% — near **historic low** |
| Access to bank finance | Improving but cost-sensitive |
| Investment intent | Recovering — 58% on growth strategies |

## Part B — ESG Survey (May 2025)

*Survey of 600 Greek SMEs. Conducted by NBG Economic Analysis Division.*

### 1. ESG Awareness & Interest

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| SMEs expressing active interest in ESG | **>66% (over two-thirds)** |
| SMEs that received substantial ESG information | **Only 22%** |
| SMEs that do NOT require mandatory regulation to act on ESG | **93%** |

> The awareness-action gap is extreme: most SMEs are interested but uninformed. This creates a significant advisory and education opportunity.

---

### 2. ESG Implementation Rates

| Category | Implementation rate |
|---|---|
| SMEs that have adopted ESG actions (overall) | **12%** |
| Very small enterprises with ESG implementation | **4%** |
| SMEs undertaking social (S) dimension actions | **7%** |
| SMEs with holistic ESG strategy (incl. governance/G) | **3%** |
| SMEs taking some environmental (E) dimension action | **~all of the 12%** |

**Interpretation:** ESG in Greek SMEs starts and mostly stays at the Environmental (E) dimension. Social and Governance dimensions are barely adopted. Implementation is nascent — even among large SMEs.

---

### 3. Primary Barriers to ESG Adoption

| Barrier | Share citing as main obstacle |
|---|---|
| Lack of knowledge / information | **~33% (one-third)** |
| Complex procedures and high costs | **Majority** cite as principal prevention factor |

---

### 4. Recognised Benefits (among those with ESG actions)

| Benefit metric | Value |
|---|---|
| SMEs with ESG reporting tangible operational benefits | **~33% (one-third)** |
| SMEs with ESG reporting satisfaction (without quantified impact) | **~66%** |
| Very small enterprises identifying measurable profitability gains | **~50% (nearly half)** |
| Hesitant firms citing operational benefits as primary adoption incentive | **~66%** |

> Profitability is the most persuasive ESG argument for Greek SMEs — especially for very small enterprises. "ESG saves you money" outperforms "ESG is the right thing to do."

---

### 5. Expected Regulatory Relief

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Estimated EU savings from regulatory simplification | **€6.3 billion** across EU |
| Mechanism | Reduced bureaucratic burden on SMEs under upcoming EU regulatory simplification package |
