## Initial Ideas Proposed

### 1) "SME Consulting Readiness" Micro-tool (Webpage)

**What it is:** A simple web-based questionnaire that outputs:
- a "readiness" score (not too gamified — more like a *profile*)
- top 3 barriers (trust, clarity, budget risk, bandwidth, etc.)
- recommended "next best step" in a **low-friction customer journey** (e.g., *short diagnostic*, *fixed-fee sprint*, *sector clinic*) aligned with what literature says SMEs respond to (trust, tangible outcomes, simplicity).

**Why it boosts the thesis:** It operationalizes the literature review logic into an artifact: "Here is how the research translates into a practical funnel."

**What you'd show in the thesis:**
- the questions + mapping logic ("If answer = X → barrier = Y → step = Z")
- screenshot of results page
- short explanation of how it supports MQ/MRQ (journey + messaging + offer design)

**MVP scope:** 1 landing page + 10–12 questions + results page (static scoring rules). No sensitive data needed.

---

### 2) "SME Journey Map Simulator" (Interactive Customer Journey)

**What it is:** An interactive journey map (webpage) where the user can click stages:
- Awareness → Consideration → Trust-building → Low-risk trial → Conversion

…and each stage shows:
- "What SMEs worry about here"
- "What message formats work here"
- "What proof reduces risk here"
- "What SMEs prefer / dislike"

This links perfectly to the thesis emphasis on **trust, relevance, plain language, and reduced risk**.

**Why it boosts the thesis:** It converts the narrative into a *visual, clickable framework* — examiners love this because it looks like a "usable model."

**MVP scope:** clickable timeline or cards; content can be drawn from the literature review + interview guide structure.

---

### 3) "Synergy Cohort Builder" Prototype (Light Clustering Concept)

**What it is:** A tool that groups SMEs into cohort types based on selected challenges:
- e.g., "Compliance-heavy", "Digital quick wins", "Cost pressure", "Growth/export intent"

…and proposes:
- which **phase** they fit into (open learning → roundtable → synergy group → 1:1)
- what a "shared package" could look like (templates, workshops, benchmarking, etc.)

**Why it boosts the thesis:** It makes the "group / cohort" idea concrete without needing confidential client data.

**MVP scope:** rule-based matching (no ML required). Use dummy SME profiles.

---

### 4) "SME Support Format Preference" Mini-Dashboard (Results Showcase)

**What it is:** A small dashboard (could be Power BI or a simple webpage chart) that visualizes:
- preferred channels (events, referrals, LinkedIn, accountants, etc.)
- preferred learning formats (workshops vs 1:1, templates vs long reports)
- trust triggers and purchase blockers

This ties directly to interview guide themes on **how SMEs hear about providers, what builds trust, and what triggers seeking external support**.

**Why it boosts the thesis:** It demonstrates the ability to present qualitative/quant insights in a decision-ready way.

---

### 5) "SME Resource Hub (Prototype)" (Content Architecture + Sample Pages)

**What it is:** A mini content hub concept (like a tiny "Insights Hub for SMEs") with:
- 3–5 sample "SME-ready" pages: checklists, explainers, "first steps"
- a simple navigation structure: "Start here" → "Common problems" → "Next step"

This fits the thesis's focus that SMEs respond better to **clarity + tangible outcomes + low friction**.

## B) The "Neural Brain" from Interview Transcripts (Knowledge Graph / Theme Network)

The "neural brain" idea becomes a **visual analytic artifact**: examiners can *see* that the analysis didn't just cherry-pick quotes — it mapped the structure.

### B1) SME Decision Network (Nodes = Themes, Edges = Co-Mentions)

**Nodes:** themes like Trust, Price/Risk, Past experiences, Referrals, "Consultant speak", Speed, Implementation, Digital readiness, Compliance fatigue, ESG uncertainty.

**Edges:** how often themes appear together in the same answer/segment (co-occurrence strength).

**Result:** a network graph where:
- big nodes = most common themes
- thick edges = strongest relationships (e.g., Trust ↔ Referrals)

---

### B2) "Barrier → Trigger → Proof" Graph (Super Thesis-Relevant)

Make the graph not just descriptive but *marketing-operational*:
- **Barrier nodes** (e.g., "too expensive", "fear of being sold to", "not for firms like us")
- **Trigger nodes** (e.g., "bank requirement", "customer pressure", "crisis", "new regulation")
- **Proof nodes** (e.g., "case evidence", "pilot", "peer referral", "sector expertise")

Edges show which barrier is typically solved by which proof (based on transcripts).

This directly supports the advice chapter and customer journey recommendations.

---

### B3) "Persona Cluster Map" from Qualitative Themes

Instead of clustering by firm size, cluster by mindset (from transcripts):
- "Cost-pressured pragmatists"
- "Growth-oriented but bandwidth-limited"
- "Compliance-driven / bank-driven"
- "Digitally curious but unsure"

Each persona is a node cluster with representative quotes attached.

---

### B4) "Network + Journey Overlay" (Killer Hybrid)

Overlay the graph onto journey stages:
- **Awareness stage:** which nodes dominate? (e.g., awareness gaps, perception)
- **Consideration:** which nodes dominate? (trust, referral, proof)
- **Purchase:** which nodes dominate? (risk, pricing, scope clarity)

This turns the graph into a **customer journey engine**.

## Who Would This Help?

### Primary Beneficiary: PwC (or Any Consulting Provider Targeting SMEs)

At its core, the platform is a **decision-support and design tool for PwC**. It helps PwC to:

- **Understand SMEs better** — aggregate *what SMEs say* (interviews) with *what is happening* (market/sector signals)
- **Design better go-to-market approaches** — translate qualitative insights into messaging principles, channel choices, low-risk entry offers, proof strategies

Indirect benefits for SMEs:
- More relevant communication (less jargon, more outcome-oriented)
- Lower perceived risk (clear scope, pilot offers, proof)
- Engagement formats that fit SME realities (clinics, workshops, diagnostics)
- Fewer "generic consulting pitches"

**Value chain: Platform → PwC insight → better engagement design → better SME experience**

### 4) Functional Requirements (Feature-by-Feature)

#### 4.1 SME Insight Dashboard (Facts Layer)

**Features:**
- Data source transparency: each chart/tile shows source + date
- "Explain this tile" → short interpretation text (not generated claims)
- Sector drill-down: show 3–5 key indicators per sector
- "Evidence link" → show how this signal appears in transcript themes (cross-panel linking)

**Required data sources (factual):**
- European Commission SME Fact Sheet Greece 2025
- OECD: Financing SMEs and Entrepreneurs 2026 (Greece chapter)
- OECD Economic Surveys: Greece 2024
- Eurostat Digitalisation in Europe 2025 / Digital intensity context
- NBG Surveys on SMEs (confidence / investment sentiment / ESG)
- Deloitte "Pulse of Greek Enterprises" (benchmark sentiment data)
- Optional: consulting market structure context

**Data ingestion requirement:**
For every source: store source_name, url, date_published/last_updated, metric_definitions; store extracted metrics with units and notes; keep raw source file link for auditability.

#### 4.2 Transcript Brain (Knowledge Graph from Interviews)

**Input:**
- Interview transcripts (text)
- Optional metadata per interview: sector, size class, role, date, channel

**Processing steps (must be explicit):**

1. **Transcription ingestion** — store raw transcript (restricted)
2. **Anonymization** — remove personal names, company names, identifiers; replace with tokens: [PERSON_A], [COMPANY_3], etc.
3. **Segmentation** — split into "quote units" (e.g., answer paragraphs or 2–5 sentence chunks)
4. **Coding / theme tagging** — either manual coding UI OR semi-automated tagging with review; output: list of themes attached to each quote unit
5. **Graph construction:**
   - Nodes: Theme, Barrier (optional subtype), Trigger, Proof signal, Persona (cluster label)
   - Edges: co-occurrence edges (themes appearing in same quote unit), directional edges for barrier→proof, stage edges (theme→journey_stage)
6. **Scoring** — node size = frequency; edge weight = co-occurrence count or normalized strength

**Graph UI requirements:**
- Pan/zoom, node hover labels, click-to-open quote drawer
- Filters: sector, size class, stage, role
- Mode toggles: Theme network / Barrier→Trigger→Proof / Persona clusters / Journey overlay

**Quote drawer requirements:**
- Show anonymized snippets
- Show which codes/themes apply
- Show metadata tags
- Link to Evidence-to-Action ("use this cluster")

#### 4.3 Ask the SME Brain (Structured Search)

**Inputs:**
- Theme index
- Quote index
- Graph edges

**Outputs for query "trust":**
- Theme definition (from codebook)
- Related themes (top N edges)
- Representative anonymized quotes (top 3)
- Distribution: which sectors/stages most common

**Search behaviour:**
- Keyword search + synonym map (thesaurus)
- Ranking: prioritize themes with higher frequency and stronger edges; allow filter-limited search

#### 4.4 Evidence-to-Action Builder (Rules Engine)

**Inputs:**
- Selected cluster: list of themes (or persona)
- Optional constraints: sector, size class, stage

**Output — Four cards:**
- Message principles
- Channels
- Entry offer type
- Proof elements

**How to implement (transparent + defensible):**
- Start with rules-based mapping (no black box):

## Does the Concept Include Field-Specific Articles & Legislation?

**Short answer: Yes — and they are a core, first-class component of the dashboard.**

### ✅ 1. Field-Specific Articles (e.g., Hospitality)

Yes — in a **curated, relevance-driven way, not as a news feed.**

When a user applies a sector filter (e.g., Hospitality / Tourism) or lands on a sector tab, the dashboard surfaces a **"Relevant Insights" module** containing:

**What appears:**
- Curated articles, reports, and briefs relevant to that sector
- Examples for hospitality: industry outlooks, operational challenges, workforce issues, cost pressure trends, digital adoption cases
- Content from: official institutions (EU, OECD), banks and economic research units, reputable industry bodies, accounting / regulatory authorities, selected high-quality thought leadership (clearly labelled)

**What does *not* happen:**
- ❌ No raw news stream
- ❌ No "latest headlines" noise
- ❌ No LinkedIn-style content dumping

Every item is:
- tagged by **sector**
- tagged by **theme** (e.g., cost, regulation, digital, ESG, workforce)
- optionally tagged by **SME size relevance**

**How articles are presented:**
- Short title
- 1–2 line "Why this matters for SMEs like you"
- Source + date (always visible)
- Link out to full content
- Optional link: "Related SME insights" → jumps to Transcript Brain themes

### ✅ 2. New Laws & Legislation Per Field

Yes — this is a **critical pillar of the dashboard**. But shown as:

**🧾 "Regulation & What It Means for Your Business" module**

For each sector, this shows **relevant new / upcoming laws and regulations** (EU-level and national-level, only those that actually affect SMEs in the selected sector).

**For each law/regulation:**
- **Name**
- **Status** (new / upcoming / in force)
- **Who it affects** (which SMEs)
- **What changes in practice**
- **What SMEs typically struggle with**
- **Typical next step** (learn / assess / comply / plan)

**Example — hospitality (illustrative):**

Instead of: *"Directive X amending Regulation Y under Article Z…"*

The dashboard shows:

> **New EU sustainability disclosure rules**
> Applies to: medium-sized hospitality businesses
> What it means: tracking energy & waste; basic reporting expectations
> Typical SME reaction: uncertainty on scope; fear of cost
> Practical first step: short readiness check before doing anything else

### ✅ 3. How Articles & Legislation Connect to the Rest of the System

Articles and laws are **not standalone content**. They are **nodes in the same system** as SME interview themes, barriers, triggers, and proof points.

So the platform can do things like:
- "This law is frequently linked to *uncertainty* and *trust issues* in SME interviews"
- "This topic often triggers engagement only after banks get involved"
- "SMEs in this sector usually respond better to clarification workshops than full projects"

### ✅ 4. Where These Appear in the UI

- ✅ On the main dashboard: Sector-specific **Insights & Articles panel** and **Regulation & Compliance panel**
- ✅ In the Evidence-to-Action Builder: Laws and articles appear as contextual justifications ("This recommendation is often relevant when X regulation applies")
- ✅ In the Transcript Brain: Laws act as **triggers**, linked to themes like confusion, urgency, cost sensitivity

### ✅ 5. Strategic Importance

SMEs don't wake up wanting consulting. They react to:
- **regulatory pressure**
- **market changes**
- **industry constraints**

By embedding field-specific articles and legislation, the platform anchors itself in **real SME life events**.

### ✅ 6. Final Concise Summary

> Yes — the concept explicitly includes both **sector-specific articles** and **sector-specific legislation** as core dashboard components. They are curated, tagged, and translated into "what this means for SMEs," and are fully connected to interview insights and action recommendations, rather than presented as raw information feeds.
